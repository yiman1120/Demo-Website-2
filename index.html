Geographical location of Japan

Geographically, Japan belongs to Northeast Asia, with the Pacific Ocean to the east, Siberia, the Korean Peninsula, and mainland China in Eurasia to the west, the Sea of Japan, the Korean Strait, and the East China Sea to the west, Taiwan and the Mariana Islands to the south, and Taiwan and the Mariana Islands to the north. The Soya Strait and the Sea of Okhotsk are adjacent to Sakhalin Island and the Kuril Islands. Japan has a comfortable and pleasant climate all year round, and the four seasons change distinctly. It is warm in the south and colder in the north. Especially in winter when the northwest monsoon is blowing, there will be a lot of snow on the Sea of Japan side. On the other hand, on the Pacific side, dry and sunny days called "winter sunshine" will continue.
